How we work

A method with seven phases and one rule.

The rule: a plan is worthless without adoption. Everything below is built to leave real capability with your people — not to keep us in the building.

The seven “In-” phases

Innovation runs through all of them.

We can join at any phase, including a project already in motion, and hand it back at any phase when your team is ready to carry it.

In·01

Innovation

Not a step so much as the posture we bring to every one. Respect what works, question what doesn't, and refuse the answer “because we've always done it that way.”

In·02

Inception

The first honest conversation. What you think the problem is, what the pressure actually feels like, and whether there's real value for us to add. If there isn't, we say so here.

In·03

Investigation

We go find the real problem, which is rarely the one on the agenda. Data, contracts, the floor, and the people who know where the bodies are buried.

In·04

Initiation

Scope, milestones, and the deliverables we agree to be judged against. A timeline you can hold us to, updated honestly when reality moves it.

In·05

Installation

The work gets built into how the place runs — new process, new terms, new leadership, new line of business. This is where most engagements quietly fail. It's the part we care about most.

In·06

Integration

Adoption. Your staff own the change, the numbers move, and the improvement survives without us standing over it.

In·07

Insight

We hand back the controls, the data, and the reasoning behind both — so your team can keep making the call after we're gone.

Getting started

The engagement process.

Step 1

A confidential first email

Everything is confidential from the first contact. Tell us the services you're interested in and what you and your organization need. The more specific, the better our first conversation. A formal RFP or RFQ is welcome but never required.

Step 2

Screening consult

A short conversation to understand the situation and confirm there's value in going further. We research what you've shared before we meet.

Step 3

Site visit, if the work needs it

Some problems can be sized over a call. Others we need to see. Where a visit helps, we'll gather what we can't get remotely.

Step 4

Proposal & quote

We lay out the approach, the areas of opportunity, and the scope. After your comments, we adapt and quote the agreed work and term.

Step 5

Contract & milestones

Services, payment schedule, and a milestone timeline for the deliverables. Then the work begins.

How we operate

Four things we hold to.

Adoption over advice

Your organization is a living thing, not brick and mortar. Nothing changes until the people doing the work own the change, so buy-in is part of the plan, not an afterthought.

Transparency on money

It's your money and you deserve to know what you're getting. We document practices and costs plainly and tell you when a forecast moves — in either direction.

We won't overstay

We're a resource at your disposal and your discretion. When you're ready to run it yourself, we hand it back. Keeping ourselves employed is not the goal.

We turn down work

If we can't add value, we won't take the engagement. That honesty early is worth more than a fee, and it's how long relationships get built.

Send one email in confidence. Name the situation and what you'd want to be different, and we'll tell you where we'd start.
