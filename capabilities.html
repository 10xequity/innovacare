What we do

Four things, done all the way through.

We keep the list short on purpose. These are the problems we know how to find, size, and repair — not a menu we read back to you.

01 / Cost

Cost takeout

There is spend in every organization that isn't buying anything anymore — it's just habit with a budget line. We go find it. Labor models built for a census you no longer have. Premium pay covering a scheduling problem. Contracts that renewed themselves for a decade. Supply spend nobody owns. Overhead that grew around a workaround.

We don't do this with a spreadsheet from a distance. We work the floor, read the contracts, and sit with the people who actually place the orders. Then we take the cost out in a way that holds after we leave — because a saving that reverses in two quarters was never a saving.

  • Labor productivity & premium-pay reduction
  • Supply chain, GPO alignment & purchased services
  • Payer, vendor & physician-group contract terms
  • Revenue cycle leakage
  • Non-clinical overhead & span-of-control
02 / Leadership

Interim & fractional leadership

A seat goes empty at the worst possible time, or a function needs a senior hand it can't yet justify full-time. We put an operator in the chair — someone who has held the role before and will run it, not warm it.

Interim, when you need the job done now: a CFO through a merger, a COO through a turnaround, a CNO steadying a floor. Fractional, when the need is real but part-time: the operating discipline a growing biotech or a smaller system wants without a permanent hire. Either way, one person is accountable to you, and the handoff to your permanent leader is part of the plan from day one.

  • Interim CFO, COO, CNO, CMO, CIO
  • Fractional executive & functional leadership
  • Program & transformation leadership
  • Leadership assessment & succession bridging
03 / Operations

Operational optimization

Operations is where strategy either happens or doesn't. We work throughput and patient flow, staffing and productivity, access and scheduling, and the P&L of individual service lines — the places where a few decisions quietly move the whole margin.

The method is unglamorous: watch how the work actually moves, find where it stalls, and fix the stall rather than the symptom. We pair that with the numbers so the change shows up in both the department and the ledger.

  • Patient flow & throughput
  • Staffing models & workforce assessment
  • Service-line performance & P&L
  • Perioperative & capacity management
  • Access, scheduling & the patient experience
04 / New models

New business models

Sometimes the fix isn't repairing what exists — it's building what should exist next. A new service line. Entry into a new market. A biotech's first commercial engine. A provider-payer arrangement that changes how the work gets paid for.

We write the business case with the same people who will have to live with it, design the operating model underneath, and then stay through the part everyone underestimates: standing it up while the rest of the organization keeps running.

  • New service-line design & launch
  • Market entry & geographic expansion
  • Biotech commercialization & market access
  • Value-based & provider-payer arrangements
  • Digital & virtual care models
