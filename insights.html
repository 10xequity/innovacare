Guide · 9 min

Where hospital cost actually hides

Most cost-reduction programs go line by line down the budget and miss the biggest money, because the biggest money isn't a line — it's a pattern. Premium pay that's really a scheduling failure. Length-of-stay that's really a discharge-planning failure. This is a map of where operating cost tends to sit and why the finance view alone won't find it.

Playbook · 7 min

Interim vs. fractional leadership

They sound similar and solve different problems. Interim is for an empty seat that needs a full-time operator now. Fractional is for a real need that doesn't yet justify a permanent hire. Choose wrong and you either overpay for capacity you don't use or under-cover a gap that keeps costing you. A short decision guide.

Field note · 11 min

Operational red flags in healthcare diligence

The data room tells you what the seller wants known. The operations tell you the rest. Staffing built on agency premium, contracts with a cliff in them, deferred maintenance dressed as EBITDA — the things that don't show up in a model but decide whether the thesis survives the first year.

Guide · 8 min

Reading a health-system P&L like an operator

An accountant reads a P&L to close the books. An operator reads it to find the two decisions that move it. How to trace a margin problem from the statement back to the department, the shift, and the choice that actually caused it.

Article · 10 min

What biotech teams get wrong about the first commercial build-out

Great science doesn't translate into a commercial org by osmosis. The common failure is hiring the expensive senior commercial leader first and building backward from their preferences. There's a better order, and it starts with market access, not headcount.

Field note · 6 min

Throughput math: how patient flow moves margin

Capacity you already own is the cheapest capacity you'll ever add. A worked example of how a handful of flow decisions — discharge timing, bed assignment, OR scheduling — free up real volume without a single new square foot.

Playbook · 9 min

Standing up a new service line without stalling the ones you have

New lines fail less on the business case than on the launch, when they quietly starve the existing operation of attention and staff. How to sequence a build so the new thing grows and the old things keep running.

Playbook · 7 min

The first 90 days of a good interim

A strong interim leader resists the urge to change everything in week one. What the first ninety days should actually contain: what to stabilize immediately, what to leave alone, and what to hand your permanent hire so they inherit momentum, not a mess.
