Who we work with

Three worlds, one operating instinct.

Health systems, biotech, and the investors behind both. The problems rhyme even when the org charts don't — and we carry what we learn from one into the next.

Sector 01

Providers & health systems

Hospitals, ambulatory networks, and academic centers carrying margin pressure, workforce strain, and a transition they can't afford to get wrong. The classic bind: no slack to fix the thing that's eating the slack.

We come in on the operational and financial side and, when the seat is empty, in the chair itself. We've converted underused capacity into new access, renegotiated the contracts that set the margin, built and repaired service lines, and run the multi-year programs that leadership doesn't have the bandwidth to carry alone.

  • Margin & labor turnaround
  • Interim executive cover
  • Service-line build & repair
  • Facility activation & transition
  • Payer & physician-group negotiation
Sector 02

Biotech & life sciences

The hardest crossing in the industry is the one from science to a business. A company that has run on discovery discipline suddenly needs commercial discipline — and the two don't think the same way.

We help life-science teams build the commercial engine before it's urgent: the market-access strategy, the operating model, the first commercial hires made in the right order. For earlier-stage companies, fractional leadership gives that discipline without the cost of a full bench too soon.

  • Commercial readiness & launch planning
  • Market access & pricing strategy
  • Operating model & org design
  • Fractional commercial & operations leadership
  • Provider & system go-to-market
Sector 03

Healthcare private equity

Two moments matter most: before you buy, and the first year after. We work both. In diligence, we read the operations the data room doesn't show — the staffing reality, the contract exposure, the fixes a seller deferred. Post-close, we help turn the thesis into something the management team can actually run.

Because we also operate inside providers and life-science companies, our diligence isn't theoretical. We've been the ones who had to make the plan work on the other side.

  • Operational & commercial due diligence
  • 100-day & value-creation plans
  • Interim leadership for portfolio companies
  • Cost & margin programs post-close
  • Add-on integration support
Across all three

The best ideas we bring to a health system usually came from a biotech. The sharpest questions we ask in diligence came from having to answer them as an operator. Moving between these worlds is the advantage — not a coincidence.
